The Making of Sequel Patterson

A story of character, kindness, and athletic excellence

GC

GameDay Carolina Staff

December 15, 2024

4 min read

Rodriguez Patterson likes to tell the story of when his son Sequel was just six years old, lying on the ground after being leveled in a youth football Super Bowl. While other kids stayed down, Sequel ran straight at him. He got flattened, stayed down for a moment, then looked up at his dad and said it didn't hurt. His Ravens team went on to win the title. For Rodriguez, that was the moment he knew: his son was different.

By the time Sequel arrived at Indian Land Middle School as an eighth grader, the word "different" didn't quite do him justice. On the court, he was dominant—so much so that he earned a spot on the varsity basketball team as a middle schooler, an almost unheard-of accomplishment.

Standing Out on the Court

His athletic director, Coach White, saw the qualities that set him apart immediately. "He wanted to dominate you between the lines," White said, "but he also wanted to dap you up after the game. That's rare."

"He wanted to dominate you between the lines, but he also wanted to dap you up after the game. That's rare."
- Coach White, Athletic Director

What truly made him stand out, though, wasn't just his athleticism or his competitive fire. It was his character. Sequel could have surrounded himself with the most popular group of students in school and offered him clout—but he didn't. Instead, he'd move tables in the cafeteria to sit with a student eating alone. His magnetism wasn't just about talent. It was about kindness.

A Different Kind of Greatness

Sequel Patterson isn't just another rising star. He's a reminder that greatness isn't defined by points scored or games won—it's defined by how you treat people when the lights dim and the crowd goes home.

The Making of a Champion

From that six-year-old who got back up after being flattened in youth football to the middle schooler who chose kindness over popularity, Sequel Patterson's journey is just beginning. But one thing is already clear: he's not just building a legacy on the court or field—he's building one in the hearts of everyone he meets.

As Rodriguez Patterson knew from that moment when his six-year-old son got back up and said it didn't hurt, Sequel was different. Now we all get to watch that difference make a difference in the lives of others.
